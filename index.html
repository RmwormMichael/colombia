Location

Colombia is one of the most biodiverse countries in the world, ranking first in diversity of birds and butterflies, and second in diversity of plants and amphibians. This richness is due to its geographical location, which includes a variety of ecosystems, from rainforests to mountains and coasts.

Colombia has several key biodiversity areas. Here I present some of the most important ones:

1. Chiribiquete National Natural Park:
Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, this park is home to stunning landscapes and a wide variety of species.

2. Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta:
The highest coastal mountain in the world, rising from sea level to more than 5,700 meters.

3. Amazonía Colombiana:
Part of the vast Amazon basin, rich in biodiversity and indigenous culture.

4. Tayrona National Natural Park:
Located on the Caribbean coast, it combines beaches, mountains and tropical jungle.

5. Chocó Region:
One of the rainiest areas on the planet, extending from the Pacific coast to the Andean region.

6. Paramos of Colombia:
High mountain ecosystems that are above the forest line, vital for water supply.

7. Eastern Plains:
Wide plains that extend to the east, with savanna and wetland ecosystems.

8. Rosario and San Bernardo Islands:
Archipelagos in the Colombian Caribbean, known for their beautiful coral reefs.

Typical Dishes

  • Ajiaco | In Colombian cuisine, soups occupy a privileged place.
  • Bandeja paisa | It is a characteristic dish of the Antioch area.
  • Sancocho | Its recipe varies depending on the region. It is a broth that is prepared with different types of meat
  • Fritanga | It is a popular dish made up of several products such as yellow potatoes or creole potatoes, stuffed sweet potatoes, chicharrón, pork ribs, chorizo, capon, stuffed or blood sausage, and pork loin.
  • Lechona | Se prepara rellenando el cerdo sin retirarle la piel con arvejas, arroz y especias.
  • Tamal | They are made with rice, flour, pork and chicken, carrots, eggs, potatoes and peas.
  • Empanadas | They contain a stew that mixes onions, ripe tomatoes, garlic, spices and seasonings, although there are many types of fillings.
  • Arepas | Made mainly with corn or wheat flour, some of them are: paisa, boyacense, corn, egg, santandereana, stuffed, cheese, among others.
  • Caldo de costilla | It is a water-based soup that contains potatoes, cilantro and beef ribs.
  • Buñuelos | They are fried balls made with cheese and corn flour.

Customs

  • Barranquilla Carnival | It is the second largest carnival in the world and the most important in Colombia. It was declared a Masterpiece of the Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity.
  • Black and White Carnival | It is one of the Colombian traditions of native Andean and pre-Hispanic custom.
  • Vallenato legend festival | The festival seeks to preserve the four airs or rhythms of vallenato: stroll, merengue, son and puya. In addition, the genre of piquería, parranda, peasant poetry, stories, legends, myths, oral tradition, literary, sociocultural and artistic expressions associated with vallenato.
  • flower fair | It is the most important event in Medellín. The fair lasts 10 days and is part of the Colombian cultural heritage.
  • Cali Fair | The Cali Fair is the most important event in that city and is a symbol of cultural identity. Every year, between December 25 and 30, the passion for life, salsa and joy is celebrated.
  • Popayán Holy Week Processions | The Popayán processions are part of the most important customs of the religious sphere in Colombia.

What should be done and what should not be done?

To do

  • Respect local culture: Familiarize yourself with the traditions and customs of each region.
  • Try the gastronomy: Don't miss typical dishes such as arepas, tumba paisa and sancocho.
  • Greet cordially: A friendly greeting is important; a “hello” or “good morning” is well received.
  • Be friendly and open: Colombians are usually very welcoming and friendly.

What not to do

  • Making negative comments about the situation in the country: The history of Colombia is complex, and it is better to avoid derogatory comments.
  • Ignoring safety warnings: Educate yourself about areas to avoid and follow local recommendations.
  • Taking photos without permission: Always ask before photographing people, especially in indigenous communities.
  • Disregard local norms: Respect rules and regulations, especially in public places.
